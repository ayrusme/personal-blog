Professional Journey

My path began in programming, where I found a certain peace in the logic of code. That journey led me to my current role as an Engineering Manager at Yellow.ai, where I lead a team of sixteen engineers. My passion for complex problems hasn't changed, but the focus has. I now apply my background in system design and software engineering not just to a codebase, but to building a cohesive and effective team.

I lead as a hands-on technical leader, staying close to the work to guide architectural decisions and help clear roadblocks. But the real measure of success has shifted. It's no longer just about a clean deployment or an elegant solution. It's about watching someone on my team master a difficult domain or step into their own potential as a leader. Those are the moments that truly matter.

Leadership Philosophy

My leadership philosophy is built on two core beliefs. First, I believe in servant leadership: my primary role is to create an environment where talented engineers can do their best work. I focus on clarifying the "what" and the "why" and then trust the team with the "how". I empower them with the autonomy to own their tasks, make technical decisions, and take pride in the outcome.

Second, I believe that growth is the goal. This is the transformational side of my approach. Autonomy is paired with deliberate mentorship, creating a space where people can build on their strengths and tackle their weaknesses. Psychological safety is just as important as technical skill. Team members must feel safe to experiment, to ask questions, and even to fail. My job is to foster a culture where learning, high performance, and personal growth all coexist.
